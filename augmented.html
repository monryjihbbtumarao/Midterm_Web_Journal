AUGMENTED REALITY

  • AR overlays digitally created content into the user’s real-world environment.
  • an enhanced version of the real physical world that is achieved through the use of digital visual elements, sound, or other sensory stimuli and delivered via technology.

VIRTUAL REALITY


the computer-generated simulation of a three-dimensional image or environment that can be interacted with in a seemingly real or physical way by a person using special electronic equipment, such as a helmet with a screen inside or gloves fitted with sensors.


MIXED REALITY


Mixed Reality is made of different advanced technologies, primarily Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality.

an expanded version of augmented reality where users can interact with virtual objects


THE ARCHITECTURE OF AUGMENTED SYSTEMS


SENSORS


  1. provide information about the user's location, orientation, and movement.
  2. AR requires sensors to detect and track the user's physical environment, such as cameras, GPS, and accelerometers.

PROCESSING


  1. The sensor data is processed by a computer or mobile device, which uses algorithms to create a virtual representation of the physical environment
  2. This representation is often referred to as a "point cloud," which is a 3D map of the environment

RENDERING


  1. Once the point cloud is generated, it must be rendered onto the user's device in real-time.
  2. This requires a powerful graphics processing unit (GPU) to create high-quality, realistic images

DISPLAY


  1. The rendered images are then displayed on the user's device, which can be a smartphone, tablet, or AR headset.
  2. The display must be capable of overlaying the virtual information onto the physical environment in a way that is seamless and natural.

USER INTERFACE


  1. AR applications require a user interface that allows the user to interact with the virtual content.
  2. This can be accomplished through touchscreens, voice commands, gestures, or other input methods.

CONTENT


  1. AR applications require content, such as 3D models, animations, and text, to be overlaid onto the physical environment.
  2. This content can be pre-loaded or generated in real-time based on the user's actions and interactions.

APPLICATION OF AR SYSTEM


  1. AR IN GAMING

    • AR games such as Pokémon Go and Harry Potter: Wizards Unite allow users to interact with virtual objects in the real world.


  2. AR IN EDUCATION

    • Creates immersive and interactive learning experiences by simulating experiments, creating virtual field trips, and enhancing textbooks.

  3. AR IN MARKETING

    • Creates engaging campaigns by blurring the lines between the physical and digital worlds, such as interactive billboards and product demos.

  4. AR IN HEALTHCARE

    • creates medical simulations that help doctors and nurses practice procedures and assist patients in understanding their conditions.
